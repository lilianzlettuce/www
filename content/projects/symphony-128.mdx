      This work utilizes self-collected social interaction data to examine the liberating yet objectifying effect of modern day communication technology on social connection. The temporal evolution of the data directly influences a dynamic rendering derived from its features (such as timestamp, duration, and in-person vs online interaction). Although these features are initially easily recognizable, the algorithm manipulates the visualization until it is no longer human-readable. The resulting images create a warped, artificial representation of something inherently human, mirroring the nature of online spaces.
