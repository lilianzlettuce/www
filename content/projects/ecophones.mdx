Ecophones is a sound art installatioon focusing on the acoustic presence or absence of bird song as indicators of ecosystem health, cultural imagination, and the history of avian listening. Three rotary phones (Ecophones) connected to web-based sound streams enable visitors to listen remotely to mediated soundscapes in real-time.

Ecophones 01 and 02 give audiences access to current bird calls and songs on the Birdweather network (https://www.birdweather.com). Birdweather is an uneven network of listening stations spread around the world, connecting sites at the intersection of citizen science, bird habitats and AI. Ecophone 01 allows the audience to dial a phone system country code, connect to a related Birdweather station, and listen to the most recently recorded bird vocaliza0on in that loca0on (see a demonstration video at: https://youtu.be/JUfIJFQvKKM). Ecophone 02 allows specific bird species to call the audience whenever a new recording of this species has been added to the Birdweather database (see a video demonstration at: https://youtu.be/4p0sYG4CMvU).

The listening experience of Ecophone 03 is based on a modified sound stream from the locus sonus sound map (https://locusonus.org/soundmap/). The phone connects to a hybrid sound space that blends AI generated bird calls and songs with the real-time sound stream of a streambox installed at the stratozero.net art space in southern Germany. In addition to the natural soundscape from a nearby forest, this streambox also captures the sound of trains passing by at regular intervals. Sonic detection of a passing train triggers a “sound particles” contributed from artists around the world, such as a chorus of AI- generated bird calls. Conceptually, we consider these collections of individual sounds as particle trails following the trains that then mix with the real-time sounds in the physical location where they are detected.
