      <br/>

      This piece is a sonic intervention of the data center beneath Purdue’s math building, which houses the university’s high-performance computing servers. On a broader scale, these types of data centers are what power the modern conveniences we take for granted. However, they are simultaneously an immense drain on resources— large companies like Google consume more energy than a small country. I wanted to delve deeper into the intentionally hidden layer of natural resources that these machines consume through an ambient mixture of environmental sounds (e.g. running water, whale calls) and machinery noises (e.g. excavation, deep sea drilling). 

      The resulting composition evokes a sense of unearthing and mining of raw materials while also embodying the machinery itself as a living, evolving entity. It contextualizes these massive man-made networks with the source of their power and raises questions about the necessity of destruction that precedes creation.
